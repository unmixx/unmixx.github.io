UNMIXX: Untangling Highly Correlated Vocals in Multiple Singing Voices Separation

Anonymous Authors
Affiliation withheld for review

Abstract

In this paper, we introduce UNMIXX, a novel framework for the multiple singing voices separation (MSVS). While simi- lar to speech separation, MSVS presents unique challenges, namely data scarcity and highly correlated nature of singing voice. To address these issues, we propose three key com- ponents: (1) a data-mining strategy to construct highly corre- lated training mixtures, (2) a reverse attention that drives the two outputs apart, down-weighting regions where their rep- resentations are highly similar and (3) a magnitude penalty loss penalizing energy erroneously assigned to the other out- put. Experiments show that UNMIXX achieves substantial improvements, with more than 2.2 dB SDRi gains on Med- leyVox evaluation set over prior method.
