UNMIXX: Untangling Highly Correlated Vocals in Multiple Singing Voice Separation

Jihoo Jung, Ji-Hoon Kim, Doyeop Kwak, Junwon Lee, Juhan Nam, Joon Son Chung
Korea Advanced Institute of Science and Technology, South Korea

Abstract

In this paper, we introduce UNMIXX, a novel framework for multiple singing voice separation (MSVS). While related to speech separation, MSVS presents unique challengesâ€”namely data scarcity and the highly correlated nature of singing voices. To address these issues, we propose three key components: (1) a musically informed mixing strategy to construct highly correlated training mixtures, (2) a reverse attention mechanism that drives the two outputs apart via cross-attention, and (3) a magnitude-penalty loss that penalizes energy erroneously assigned to the other output. Experiments show that UNMIXX achieves substantial improvements, with more than ~2.2 dB SDRi gains on the MedleyVox evaluation set over prior methods. Audio samples are available below.
